The Amazon Firestick is one of the most popular streaming devices, and it works brilliantly with StreamVault. This guide walks you through the complete setup process in under 5 minutes.

## What You'll Need

- Amazon Firestick (2nd generation or later; 4K or 4K Max recommended)
- Stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps for HD, 25 Mbps for 4K)
- Active StreamVault subscription with login credentials

## Step-by-Step Installation

### Step 1: Enable Developer Options

From the Firestick home screen, navigate to **Settings > My Fire TV > Developer Options**. If you don't see Developer Options, go to **Settings > My Fire TV > About** and click on the Fire TV Stick name seven times to enable it.

### Step 2: Allow Apps from Unknown Sources

In Developer Options, enable **"Apps from Unknown Sources"** or **"Install unknown apps"**. This is required to install apps not available on the Amazon App Store.

### Step 3: Install the Downloader App

Go to the Amazon App Store and search for **"Downloader"** by AFTVnews. Install it — this free app allows you to download APK files directly to your Firestick.

### Step 4: Download StreamVault

Open the Downloader app and enter the APK URL provided in your StreamVault welcome email. The download will begin automatically. Once complete, the installer will prompt you to install the app.

### Step 5: Launch and Log In

Open the StreamVault app, enter your username and password, and you're ready to stream. Browse through 20,000+ channels, movies, and series in stunning 4K quality.

## Pro Tips for Firestick Users

- **Use an Ethernet adapter** for the most stable connection, especially for 4K content
- **Clear cache regularly** via Settings > Applications > Manage Installed Applications
- **Restart your Firestick** weekly for optimal performance
- **Enable Data Monitoring** to track your bandwidth usage
